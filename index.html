Acquired Antiquities

The beginnings of Acquired Antiquities started as a childhood hobby. I vividly remember the cold New England Saturday mornings traversing the city with my mother searching for the "Great Find". I developed a deep appreciation for items of yesteryear. Researching and discovering the history of the item is just as interesting, if, not more than, determining its collectibility value.

Tuskegee Airman - Photograph

Of all the Acquired Antiquities, the greatest is my inherited love for flying. I am forever indebted to my late father, Tuskegee Flight Officer, Robert Otis Merriweather for encouraging me to pursue and to complete my instrument rating. A native Alabamian, Robert Merriweather, attended segregated schools. He excelled in mathematics. Out of necessity, the Air Corp opened its doors to African American men. During academic training, Mr. Merriweather's mathematical gift was recoghized. Consequently, he was selected to participate in flight training. We must never forget their contribution to aviation.

Francisco Goya - "Los Disparates" or "Los Proverbios" Etchings

Acquired during a leisure peruse through the 46th Street Salvation Army Thrift Store, this set of L'Art: Revue Hebdomadaire Illustrée books were found to contain the first publication of four Francisco Goya plates. As a knowledgeable art collector, I knew I found a special collection. Listed below is the accompanying auction description. [GOYA Y LUCIENTES, FRANCISCO DE.] L'Art: Revue Hebdomadaire Illustrée. Paris: A. Ballue, 1876-1881. 11 volumes. Illustrated with numerous engraved, etched, and lithographed plates, including 4 etchings with aquatint (240 x 350 mm) by Goya in Troisiéme Année, Tome II, 1877. Folio (440 x 305 mm). Half roan, giltstamped, binding defective. With five additional volumes, art history. The first publication of four plates by Goya: "Lluvia de Toros," "Otras Leyes Por El Pueblo," "Que Guerrero," and "Una Reina del Circo." Part of the group of 22 etchings known as "Los Disparates" or "Los Proverbios," of which 18 had previously been published in 1864. Harris 266-9. Delteil 220-3.

Lt. Comdr. I. Schlossbach - Letter

Schlossbach went on twelve polar expeditions, three to the Arctic and nine to the Antarctic. He was on the Wilkins Trans-Arctic Expedition in 1931 and served as navigator on the USS Nautilus, the first attempt to take a submarine to the North Pole under the icepack. He commanded Admiral Richard Byrd's ship the Bear of Oakland and was a pilot on Byrd's Second Antarctic Expedition (1933 35). He was the second in command on the MacGregor Arctic Expedition (1937 38) where he accomplished a number of polar aviation firsts. In 1939, he accompanied Byrd again to the Antarctic on the United States Antarctic Service Expedition.

George Washington Carver - Signature

George Washington Carver was born into slavery in Diamond, Missouri, around 1864. The exact year and date of his birth are unknown. Carver went on to become one of the most prominent scientists and inventors of his time, as well as a teacher at the Tuskegee Institute. Carver devised over 100 products using one major crop the peanut including dyes, plastics and gasoline. He died in 1943.

Mezuzah - Antique Pendant

A small parchment scroll upon which the Hebrew words of the Shema are handwritten by a scribe. Mezuzah scrolls are rolled up and affixed to the doorposts of Jewish homes, designating the home as Jewish and reminding those who live there of their connection to G d and their heritage.
